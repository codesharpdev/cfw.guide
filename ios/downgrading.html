iOS Downgrading Guide

Downgrading to iOS 8.4.1 (32-bit only)

Downgrade requires Jailbreak and Cydia already installed

  1. Backup your iDevice through iTunes in case anything goes wrong
  2. Download Filza from Cydia
  3. Navigate to /System/Library/CoreServices
  4. Open SystemVersion.plist
  5. Change ProductVersion to 6.0
    • iPad mini and iPad 4 require 6.1
  6. Change ProductBuildVersion to one of the following
    7. 10A403 10A405 10A406 10B141
    iPad 2 iPhone 5 iPod Touch 5 iPad 4
    iPad 3 iPad mini 1
    iPhone 4
    iPhone 4S
    iPod Touch 4
  7. Reboot your device
  8. Go to Settings > General > Software Update
  9. Once iOS 8.4.1 appears, select Download and install
    • If it doesn't, try again to see where you went wrong

Downgrading to iOS 6.1.3 (32-bit only)

Downgrade requires Jailbreak and Cydia already installed
Beehind requires Windows

  1. Backup your iDevice through iTunes; This will wipe your iPhone
  2. Download and install OpenSSH from Cydia
  3. Download and extract Beehind 0.5
  4. Download the 6.1.3 IPSW for your device from ipsw.me
  5. Open Beehind as administrator
  6. Under Browse for the IPSW, click Choose and select the IPSW you just downloaded
  7. Click Yes
  8. Connect your iDevice to your computer and click New Apple Device Found! Click here to get its ECID
  9. Click Yes
  10. Check the Jailbreak and Install Cydia boxes if you want to jailbreak your iOS 6 install
  11. Click Build IPSW and wait for it to finish
  12. You will be redirected to the DFU Pwner page
    • Make sure you installed OpenSSH via Cydia
    • Make sure your iDevice is still connected to your PC
  13. Click Enter Pwned DFU Mode
    • If Windows throws an error, find the iDevice's IP address through Settings > Wifi
    • Click the i button next to your Wifi connection
    • Write the IP address down in the box in Beehind
    • Click Enter Pwned DFU Mode again
  14. You will be redirected to the idevicerestore page
  15. Click Restore

Once it's finished, your device should now be on iOS 6.1.3!
