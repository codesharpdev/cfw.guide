SD2Vita

Use an Micro SD card instead of those expensive memory cards

For this guide, you will need:

  • A Computer
  • A Vita on FW 3.60
  • SD2Vita Drivers
  • zzBlank.img file

Instructions

  1. Back up your PS Vita memory card via FTP or USB transfer
  2. Move your ux0:tai folder to ur0:tai
    • Make sure that any plugins in the tai folder are edited to ur0: in the config.txt file
  3. Copy gamesd.skprx to ur0:tai
  4. Add the following to ur0:tai/config.txt
    *KERNEL
    ur0:tai/gamesd.skprx
  5. Put the microSD card into your computer
  6. Follow these next steps with a Windows, Mac or Linux computer

Windows Instructions

  1. Download Win32 Disk Imager
  2. Open Win32 Disk Imager and select zzBlank.img from your computer
  3. Select your microSD and click Write
  4. Take out your microSD card and put it back in again
  5. Format your disk from Windows
    • File System: exFAT
    • Allocation Unit Size: Default Allocation Size
    • Do not put a volume lable
  6. Copy the backup you made earlier to the SD Card
  7. Put the SD Card into the SD2Vita adapter and reboot your Vita

Mac Instructions

  1. Go to Disk Utility > Select Card > Partition > Options > MBR > 1 partiton > exFAT
  2. Copy the backup you made earlier to the SD Card
  3. Put the SD Card into the SD2Vita adapter and reboot your Vita

Linux Instructions

  1. Find the whole-device node (usually /dev/sdx)
    • The mount command may be of help.
  2. Unmount all partitions, but don't eject the disk
    • umount /dev/sdx
  3. Use the dd command to write zzBlank to the card
    • dd if=/path/to/zzBlank.img of=/dev/what-you-found-above
  4. Take out the microSD card and put it back in again
  5. Create a new MBR (msdos) partition table, an exFAT partition, and format that partition
    • mkfs.exfat /dev/sdx
  6. Copy the backup you made earlier to the SD Card
  7. Put the SD Card into the SD2Vita adapter and reboot your Vita

Now, whenever HENkaku is launched, the SD card will automatically mount.
If you have HENkaku Enso installed, it will automatically mount on boot.

Help

Q: My SD2Vita adapter is stuck in my Vita! How do I get it out?
A: Use a toothpick or another thin object to gently pry it out.

Q: My SD2Vita adapter keeps popping out of my Vita! How do I get it in?
A: Apply a small bit of tape to the side of the adapter. This will give it the extra thickness it needs to stay in.
