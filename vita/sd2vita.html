Vita Guide

SD2Vita

Use an Micro SD card instead of those expensive memory cards

For this guide, you will need:

  • A Computer
  • A Vita on FW 3.60
  • An FTP Client (WinSCP/Filezilla should do the trick!)
  • Win32 Disk Imager
  • SD2Vita Drivers
  • zzBlank.img file

Instructions

  1. Back up your PS Vita memory card
  2. Move your "ux0:tai" folder to "ur0:tai"
  3. Copy "gamesd.skprx" to "ur0:tai"
  4. Add the following to "ur0:tai/config.txt"
    *KERNEL
    ur0:tai/gamesd.skprx
  5. Put the microSD card into your computer
  6. Follow these next steps with a Windows, Mac or Linux computer

Windows Instructions

  1. Open Win32 Disk Imager and select zzBlank.img from your computer
  2. Select your microSD and click "Write"
  3. Take out your microSD card and put it back in again
  4. Format your disk from Windows
    • File System: exFAT
    • Allocation Unit Size: Default Allocation Size
    • Do not put a volume lable
  5. Copy the backup you made earlier to the SD Card
  6. Put the SD Card into the SD2Vita adapter and reboot your Vita

Mac Instructions

  1. Go to Disk Utility > Select Card > Partition > Options > MBR > 1 partiton > exFAT
  2. Copy the backup you made earlier to the SD Card
  3. Put the SD Card into the SD2Vita adapter and reboot your Vita

Linux Instructions

  1. Find the whole-device node (usually /dev/sd?, /dev/rdisk?, /dev/mmcblk? or some variant on the theme - check out the documentation of your OS, and be especially careful to use the correct disk!) The "mount" command may be of help.
  2. Make sure to unmount all partitions, but not eject the disk
  3. Use the dd command to write zzBlank to the card (dd if=/path/to/zzBlank.img of=/dev/what-you-found-above)
  4. Eject and reinsert the card
  5. Create a new MBR partition table, an exFAT partition, and format that partition
  6. Copy the backup you made earlier to the SD Card
  7. Put the SD Card into the SD2Vita adapter and reboot your Vita

Thanks to GBAtemp User Ryccardo for providing steps for Mac and Linux.

Help

Q: My SD2Vita adapter is stuck in my Vita! How do I get it out?
A: Use a toothpick or another thin object to gently pry it out.

Q: My SD2Vita adapter keeps popping out of my Vita! How do I get it in?
A: Apply a small bit of tape to the side of the adapter. This will give it the extra thickness it needs to stay in.
