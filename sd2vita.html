SD2Vita

Use an Micro SD card instead of those expensive memory cards

For this guide, you will need a:

  • A Computer
  • A Vita on FW 3.60
  • An FTP Client (WinSCP/Filezilla should do the trick!)
  • Win32 Disk Imager
  • SD2Vita Drivers
  • zzBlank.img file

    • Windows Instructions

    Only for Windows Vista and above
    Windows XP should work though

    1. Back up your PS Vita memory card
    2. Move your "ux0:tai" folder to "ur0:tai"
    3. Copy "gamesd.skprx" to "ur0:tai"
    4. Add the following to "ur0:tai/config.txt"
      *KERNEL
      ur0:tai/gamesd.skprx
    5. Put the microSD card into your computer
    6. Open Win32 Disk Imager and select zzBlank.img from your computer
    7. Select your microSD and click "Write"
    8. Take out your microSD card and put it back in again
    9. Format your disk from Windows
    10. File System: exFAT
    11. Allocation Unit Size: Default Allocation Size
    12. Do not put a volume lable
    13. Copy the backup you made earlier to the SD Card
    14. Put the SD Card into the SD2Vita adapter and reboot your Vita

    Mac Instructions

  • Back up your PS Vita memory card
  • Move your "ux0:tai" folder to "ur0:tai"
  • Copy "gamesd.skprx" to "ur0:tai"
  • Add the following to "ur0:tai/config.txt"
    *KERNEL
    ur0:tai/gamesd.skprx
  • Put the microSD card into your computer
  • Go to Disk Utility > Select Card > Partition > Options > MBR > 1 partiton > exFAT
  • Copy the backup you made earlier to the SD Card
  • Put the SD Card into the SD2Vita adapter and reboot your Vita

    • Linux Instructions

  • Back up your PS Vita memory card
  • Move your "ux0:tai" folder to "ur0:tai"
  • Copy "gamesd.skprx" to "ur0:tai"
  • Add the following to "ur0:tai/config.txt"
    *KERNEL
    ur0:tai/gamesd.skprx
  • Put the microSD card into your computer
  • Find the whole-device node (usually /dev/sd?, /dev/rdisk?, /dev/mmcblk? or some variant on the theme - check out the documentation of your OS, and be especially careful to use the correct disk!) The "mount" command may be of help.
  • Make sure to unmount all partitions, but not eject the disk
  • Use the dd command to write zzBlank to the card (dd if=/path/to/zzBlank.img of=/dev/what-you-found-above)
  • Eject and reinsert the card
  • Create a new MBR partition table, an exFAT partition, and format that partition
  • Copy the backup you made earlier to the SD Card
  • Put the SD Card into the SD2Vita adapter and reboot your Vita

    • Thanks to GBAtemp User Ryccardo for providing steps for Mac and Linux.

    Help

    Q: My SD2Vita adapter is stuck in my Vita! How do I get it out?
    A: Use a toothpick or another thin object to gently pry it out.

    Q: My SD2Vita adapter keeps popping out of my Vita! How do I get it in?
    A: Apply a small bit of tape to the side of the adapter. This will give it the extra thickness it needs to stay in.
